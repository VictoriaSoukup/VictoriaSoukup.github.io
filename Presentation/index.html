Their herds include around 50 to 70 animals, sometimes over 100 animals. They are led by an experienced, often particularly large and strong elephant cow. Watch out for each other in the herd of elephants. Social cohesion is extremely important. When elephant bulls become independent, they leave the herd and form “male communities”. However, they continue to see their families and the contacts remain friendly.

Pachyderm: Although elephant skin is very thick on many parts of the body, it is highly sensitive. After bathing, elephants use their trunks to blow sand and mud onto wet skin to provide natural protection from sunburn and insects.

Elephants have around 150,000 muscle units in their trunk. Their trunks are perhaps the most sensitive organ found in any mammal - Asian elephants have been seen to pick up a peanut, shell it, blow the shell out and eat the nut.
